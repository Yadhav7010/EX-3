# Synthesis of Tin Oxide (SnO₂) Nanoparticles by Chemical Method

## Aim
To synthesize tin oxide (SnO₂) nanoparticles by a chemical method.

---

## Materials Required

### Chemicals
- **Precursor:** Stannous chloride (SnCl₂)  
- **Solvent:** Distilled water  
- **Precipitating Agent:** Sodium hydroxide (NaOH)  
- **Surfactant:** Sodium dodecyl sulphate (SDS)  

### Apparatus
- Beaker  
- Conical flask  
- Measuring cylinder  
- Magnetic stirrer with hot plate  
- Burette  
- Thermometer  
- pH paper / pH meter  

---

## Formula

\[
W = N \times e \times l
\]

Where:
- **N** = Normality  
- **e** = Equivalent weight  
- **l** = Volume (in liters)  

---

## Procedure

1. **Preparation of Stannous Chloride Solution**  
   Dissolve stannous chloride (SnCl₂) in distilled water and stir well.

2. **Addition of Surfactant**  
   Add sodium dodecyl sulphate (SDS) to the solution and stir continuously.

3. **Preparation of NaOH Solution**  
   Prepare sodium hydroxide (NaOH) solution separately in distilled water.

4. **Mixing of Solutions**  
   Add NaOH solution slowly into the stannous chloride solution under continuous stirring.

5. **Formation of Precipitate**  
   Continue stirring at 60–70°C until a white precipitate forms.

6. **Filtration and Washing**  
   Filter the precipitate and wash it thoroughly with distilled water.

7. **Drying**  
   Dry the obtained sample in a hot air oven.

8. **Calcination**  
   Heat the dried powder in a furnace at 600°C to obtain SnO₂ nanoparticles.
